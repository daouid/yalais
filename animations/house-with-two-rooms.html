Global Proof Structure

The Lean proof establishes that Bing's House with Two Rooms ($Y_0$) is contractible by decomposing the solid box $Y_{24}$ into 24 cells $C_1 \dots C_{24}$ and composing their individual deformation retractions:

Y_24 → Y_23 → Y_22 → ... → Y_0

Where each intermediate subspace is defined as $Y_k = Y_{k-1} \cup C_k$. Topologically, $C_k$ is a closed 3-cell (homeomorphic to a 3D cube) that intersects the accumulated space $Y_{k-1}$ precisely along $F_k$, a contractible union of 4 or 5 of its boundary faces (homeomorphic to a 2D disk).

Because the intersection is contractible, the deformation retraction $C_k \to F_k$ extends to a global deformation retraction of $Y_k$ onto $Y_{k-1}$. By composition, the entire solid box $Y_{24}$ deformation retracts onto Bing's House $Y_0$. Since the solid box is convex and contractible, Bing's House is contractible.
